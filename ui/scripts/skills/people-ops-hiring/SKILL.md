# People Ops Hiring

Use this skill when the work spans recruiting plus people operations, not just candidate outreach. It is optimized for calibrated hiring, structured interviews, onboarding readiness, and manager-facing operating rhythms.

## When to Use

- Drafting role scorecards, leveling expectations, or hiring plans
- Designing interview loops and decision criteria
- Creating candidate communication, debrief, and offer workflows
- Building onboarding checklists for managers and new hires
- Auditing process bottlenecks, funnel conversion, or calibration drift
- Writing manager enablement and employee lifecycle practices

## Workflow

1. Define the role before sourcing.
   Capture scope, outcomes, must-have signals, and non-goals.
2. Build a calibrated interview loop.
   Each interviewer should own a distinct signal, question set, and written scorecard.
3. Measure the funnel.
   Track source quality, pass-through rates, time-in-stage, and offer acceptance.
4. Design the first 30 days.
   Assign manager expectations, onboarding milestones, and early feedback checkpoints.
5. Close the learning loop.
   Feed hiring misses, ramp failures, and candidate feedback back into role design.

## Output Shape

- Role brief: outcomes, scope, must-have signals, anti-signals
- Interview plan: stages, owners, evidence expected, decision rule
- Funnel review: conversion, bottlenecks, sourcing mix, calibration notes
- Onboarding plan: first-week, first-month, stakeholder map, risks
- Manager note: expectations, coaching cadence, escalation path

## Heuristics

- Use evidence-based scorecards rather than unstructured impressions.
- A fast process is valuable only if calibration stays explicit.
- Hiring quality depends on role clarity more than sourcing volume.
- Onboarding should prove role success, not only paperwork completion.

## Anti-Patterns

- Sourcing before the role is clearly defined
- Reusing the same interview loop for unrelated roles
- Making hiring decisions without written evidence
- Treating onboarding as HR-only instead of manager-owned
