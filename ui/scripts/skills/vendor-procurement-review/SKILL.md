# Vendor Procurement Review

Use this skill when the decision is about vendor fit, renewal risk, and control quality rather than broad legal theory. It is optimized for finance, procurement, compliance, and legal workflows that need consistent approval criteria.

## When to Use

- Reviewing a new vendor request or renewal packet
- Comparing suppliers across security, cost, and contract terms
- Preparing approval memos for finance or legal signoff
- Auditing spend controls, payment terms, or renewal exposure
- Checking whether a vendor requires security, privacy, or DPA review
- Recommending negotiation points before signature or renewal

## Workflow

1. Frame the purchase.
   Identify business owner, spend amount, renewal date, data sensitivity, and implementation dependency.
2. Separate must-pass checks.
   Security, privacy, legal, and budget constraints are gating criteria, not weighted preferences.
3. Evaluate commercial shape.
   Inspect pricing model, usage cliffs, auto-renewal language, payment timing, and exit costs.
4. Document the decision.
   Produce a recommendation with required approvals, negotiation points, and unresolved risks.
5. Set review timing.
   Capture renewal checkpoint dates, owner, and evidence required before extension.

## Output Shape

- Vendor summary: purpose, owner, budget, contract term
- Gating checks: security, privacy, finance, legal, procurement status
- Commercial risks: pricing, lock-in, renewal, termination, liability
- Recommendation: approve, approve with conditions, or reject
- Negotiation list: exact clauses or commercial points to press

## Heuristics

- A cheap vendor with weak exit terms is often expensive later.
- Auto-renewal and usage overages deserve explicit owner attention.
- If the vendor handles sensitive data, privacy and security review come before price negotiation.
- Renewal reviews should happen early enough to preserve leverage.

## Anti-Patterns

- Treating procurement as a price-only comparison
- Approving renewals without usage and owner validation
- Assuming the master agreement is fine because security passed
- Waiting until expiration week to start a renewal review
