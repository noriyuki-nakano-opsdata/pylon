# Lifecycle Campaign Ops

Use this skill when the job is orchestrating a repeatable campaign motion across content, channels, and follow-up. It is optimized for launch readiness, nurture logic, and cross-functional campaign execution.

## When to Use

- Turning a brief into a launch plan with owners and dates
- Designing nurture sequences across email, content, and sales follow-up
- Mapping campaign stages to asset readiness and audience segments
- Creating launch calendars and review checkpoints
- Repairing handoffs between marketing, SDR, product marketing, and sales
- Summarizing campaign performance into next actions

## Workflow

1. Define the campaign thesis.
   State audience, pain point, offer, primary CTA, and success metric.
2. Build the launch map.
   Separate briefing, asset production, QA, launch, follow-up, and review phases.
3. Assign audience and cadence.
   Define who sees what, through which channel, and in what order.
4. Bind handoffs to dates.
   Every transition from content to distribution to sales follow-up needs an owner and review date.
5. Close the loop.
   Return launch criteria, in-flight checks, and post-campaign review prompts.

## Output Shape

- Campaign thesis: audience, offer, CTA, KPI
- Launch plan: milestone, owner, due date, dependency
- Sequence map: touchpoint, audience, trigger, fallback
- Asset matrix: asset, status, owner, usage
- Review note: result, insight, next test

## Heuristics

- Campaigns fail when the CTA, audience, and follow-up owner are vague.
- One campaign should have one primary conversion goal.
- Launch QA should verify message continuity from ad or email to landing experience.
- Review loops should produce the next experiment, not only a performance recap.

## Anti-Patterns

- Mixing awareness and conversion goals in one launch plan
- Shipping assets without a clear usage map
- Sending nurture sequences without recycle rules or suppressions
- Treating campaign reporting as separate from campaign design
