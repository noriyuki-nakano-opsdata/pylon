# GTM Reporting Analytics

Use this skill when the task is translating GTM performance data into decisions, not just producing raw tables. It is optimized for executive summaries, funnel diagnosis, and next-action reporting across sales and marketing.

## When to Use

- Writing weekly or monthly GTM business reviews
- Comparing channel performance and contribution
- Explaining why funnel conversion or velocity changed
- Building KPI scorecards for leadership
- Summarizing campaign or pipeline performance into decisions
- Identifying data quality caveats before drawing conclusions

## Workflow

1. Define the business question.
   State whether the review is about pipeline, acquisition efficiency, retention, or blended GTM performance.
2. Validate data trust.
   Call out missing attribution, stale CRM fields, time-window issues, or denominator changes before interpreting trends.
3. Separate signal layers.
   Distinguish output metrics, leading indicators, and operating constraints.
4. Convert insights into decisions.
   Every report should end with actions, owner, and timing.
5. Keep the summary executive-ready.
   Start with the few numbers and findings that change what leadership should do next.

## Output Shape

- Executive summary: headline, confidence, decision implication
- KPI table: metric, current, prior, delta, commentary
- Funnel findings: stage or channel, issue, likely cause
- Caveats: data risk, attribution gap, sample-size warning
- Action list: owner, action, expected effect, review date

## Heuristics

- A report without actions is only a dashboard export.
- Leading indicators should be read before lagging revenue totals.
- Attribution caveats must be explicit when comparing channels.
- One strong narrative is better than a long list of weak metrics.

## Anti-Patterns

- Mixing pipeline creation, conversion, and retention into one vague conclusion
- Reporting deltas without naming the operational driver
- Hiding data-quality caveats below the fold
- Sending a KPI pack with no owner-level next step
