# Customer Onboarding Playbooks

Use this skill when the work is about getting customers to initial value quickly and predictably. It is designed for onboarding specialists, CSMs, TAMs, support operations, and service delivery leads.

## When to Use

- Creating kickoff agendas and implementation plans
- Defining success milestones, owners, and target dates
- Designing enablement materials and training sequences
- Building health checkpoints for activation and early adoption
- Repairing handoffs between sales, onboarding, support, and CSM
- Writing intervention plans for delayed launches or low adoption

## Workflow

1. Define the customer outcome.
   State the business result, target users, and minimum proof of value.
2. Translate outcome into milestones.
   Separate technical setup, workflow adoption, stakeholder readiness, and success review.
3. Assign owners and risks.
   Every milestone needs a customer owner, internal owner, and a known blocker path.
4. Instrument early warning signals.
   Track kickoff completion, integration status, training completion, active users, and unresolved blockers.
5. Close the handoff.
   End onboarding with a documented handoff to CSM or support, including open risks and next-quarter goals.

## Output Shape

- Success plan: objective, milestones, dates, owners, dependencies
- Activation checklist: required setup, optional accelerators, blockers
- Enablement plan: audience, format, timing, owner
- Risk register: signal, likely cause, intervention, escalation path
- Handoff note: outcomes achieved, adoption status, unresolved items

## Heuristics

- Optimize for time-to-first-value, not document completeness.
- High-touch customers need explicit executive alignment and clear milestone ownership.
- Tech-touch onboarding still needs trigger-based intervention rules.
- Training is not adoption. Require evidence of workflow usage.
- Closed-lost onboarding retrospectives should feed back into sales qualification.

## Anti-Patterns

- Treating kickoff completion as proof of success
- Handing off customers without documented risks or activation evidence
- Using the same plan for enterprise, mid-market, and SMB accounts
- Overloading customers with training before core setup is complete
