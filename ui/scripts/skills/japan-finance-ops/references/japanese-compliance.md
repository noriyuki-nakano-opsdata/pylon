# Japanese Compliance Checks

Use this reference when the task touches bookkeeping rules, invoice validation, document retention, or finance/legal approval language in Japan.

## Core Areas

- `J-GAAP`: Keep statutory bookkeeping separate from management reporting commentary.
- `Consumption tax`: State the tax treatment explicitly. Do not assume standard rate without evidence.
- `Qualified invoice system`: Verify issuer identity, invoice number, tax amount, rate buckets, and document completeness.
- `Electronic bookkeeping law`: Preserve searchable evidence, timestamps or system trail, and traceable storage location.

## Approval Questions

- Is the legal entity and counterparty name consistent across request, invoice, and contract?
- Is the owner and budget authority explicit?
- Is the contract term, renewal rule, and exit path documented?
- Is the evidence sufficient for later audit or tax review?

## Red Flags

- Missing tax rate or mixed rate handling
- Missing issuer registration or incomplete invoice metadata
- Approval request without attachment trail
- Renewal request with no usage, spend trend, or owner confirmation
