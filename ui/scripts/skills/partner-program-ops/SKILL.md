# Partner Program Ops

Use this skill when the job is operationalizing partnerships rather than negotiating one contract. It is optimized for sourcing, onboarding, tiering, referral management, MDF governance, and cadence design between sales, product, and alliances.

## When to Use

- Designing partner tiers, benefits, and entry criteria
- Building partner onboarding checklists and enablement paths
- Defining referral, reseller, and co-sell operating models
- Running joint business planning and quarterly partner reviews
- Tracking sourced, influenced, and co-sold pipeline
- Governing MDF usage and partner-sourced lead quality

## Workflow

1. Define the motion.
   State whether the partner model is referral, reseller, services, technology, or strategic alliance.
2. Define qualification and tiering.
   Set entry requirements, target segment, expected contribution, and benefits by tier.
3. Build the operational path.
   Document onboarding steps, enablement assets, lead-routing rules, and escalation paths.
4. Measure contribution correctly.
   Separate sourced, influenced, attached, and closed revenue. Do not collapse them into one number.
5. Review and tune.
   Inspect activation rates, pipeline quality, attach rates, and time-to-first-deal per partner cohort.

## Output Shape

- Program definition: motion, segment, ICP, partner profile
- Tier design: criteria, benefits, expectations, review cadence
- Onboarding plan: enablement, integrations, certifications, CRM setup
- Pipeline operating model: lead routing, attribution, acceptance SLAs
- QBR summary: wins, risks, asks, next-quarter commitments

## Heuristics

- Partner programs fail when enablement, attribution, and seller incentives disagree.
- Sourced and influenced revenue should be tracked separately.
- A partner that never activates is a recruiting problem, not a forecasting problem.
- Joint plans should include concrete pipeline and product commitments, not only relationship goals.

## Anti-Patterns

- Signing many partners before one cohort proves activation
- Crediting partner pipeline without a clear acceptance rule
- Treating MDF as spend with no measurable follow-through
- Creating benefits that internal teams cannot actually honor
